# Market Analysis Eval Strategy

## Goal

Evaluate whether `market-analysis` turns a rough opportunity idea into a market-backed view of demand, competition, and viable openings before the team spends time on feasibility or requirements.

## Why Routed Review First

This skill is not mainly a discoverability test. Its value comes from routing a real opportunity question into a usable research brief that improves the next discovery step. Review should check that the skill:

- stays on market evidence rather than drifting into product strategy or requirements
- distinguishes direct, indirect, and adjacent competitors
- makes the opportunity ranking auditable

## Scenarios

Use two review scenarios:

1. A new-product opportunity with an unclear segment, such as a workflow tool for freelance design teams.
2. An expansion idea where the market exists but the team must judge whether there is still a defensible gap.

## Assertions

1. The output defines a specific market segment instead of a vague category.
2. At least five competitors are analyzed across direct, indirect, and adjacent buckets.
3. Market gaps are backed by evidence such as reviews, forum posts, pricing pages, or survey data.
4. TAM, SAM, and SOM are estimated with explicit methodology and assumptions.
5. Opportunities are ranked using clear criteria, not intuition alone.
6. The report stops before feasibility, requirements, or architecture work begins.

## Method

Produce a baseline market memo without the skill, then produce the same memo with `market-analysis` explicitly invoked. Compare the two outputs for:

- evidence density
- competitor coverage
- ranking clarity
- whether the opportunity is framed narrowly enough to support a decision

## Exit Criteria

Keep the skill in `review` until the output is strong enough that a reviewer can point to the ranked opportunity list and understand why it won. Promote the skill to `review` only when the research is specific, evidence-backed, and clearly hands off to `feasibility-study` or `requirements-engineering`.
