## Sequencing Decisions Caused By Risk

1. Extract the shared validation service and prove exact legacy parity before adding writers (`DAR-R02`, `DAR-R03`).
2. Freeze versioned result schemas, including post-commit recovery truth, before host/authoring consumers exist (`DAR-R09`).
3. Implement local locking, safe create paths, and `state-init` recovery before general state mutation (`DAR-R04`, `DAR-R06`).
4. Land completion/retry independently of reroute so transaction complexity cannot block core adoption (`DAR-R06`, `DAR-R07`, `DAR-R08`).
5. Require gray evidence before any strict-default promotion or reconsideration of worktree scope/repinning (`DAR-R11`).
6. Commit tracked acceptance evidence before final self-host regeneration, then prohibit further tracked changes (`DAR-R13`).

## Accepted Residual Risks

- Direction 2 does not authenticate the actor supplying a route/completion pin.
- A direct writer that ignores the local advisory lock can race after the final digest check.
- Shared/network-filesystem authoring and multi-host writers are unsupported.
- Power loss, kernel panic, storage failure, and filesystem corruption are outside automatic recovery; only injected authoring-process termination is accepted.
- Self-reported timestamps are not trusted clocks.
- Rollover, archival, and compaction are unavailable until the 12 MiB evidence trigger starts a new ADR.

These risks are accepted only for opt-in, single-host Direction 2. Any requirement to remove them changes the architecture route rather than silently expanding this plan.
